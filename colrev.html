The Spirit of '76

The Spirit of '76

Colonial Farms seeks to commemorate the “Spirit of '76.” The “Spirit of '76” represents the patriotic sentiment surrounding the American Revolution and the attitude of self-determination and individual liberty for all.

Our country's mythical Founding Fathers, perhaps best exemplified by Thomas Jefferson, and the Second Continental Congress incorporated the principles of the “Spirit of '76” into the Declaration of Independence on July 4, 1776.

These principles include the self-evident truths of being created equal and being endowed by their Creator with certain inalienable rights including life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness.

The “Spirit of '76” painting by Archibald Willard, also known as "Yankee Doodle", depicts this patriotic spirit quite well. The painting portrays three soldiers of the American Revolution. Though one is wounded, the soldiers march on with spirit and determination.

Indeed, the Colonial Farms endeavors to keep alive some of this “Spirit of '76.” In fact, the Farm's logo incorporates Willard's “Spirit of '76” painting.

Colonial Revival

The Colonial Revival movement emerged in 1876 with America's Centennial celebration. Historic preservation formed the core of the Colonial Revival movement. As a result, there was a growing interest in preservation of historic properties. Private individuals and preservation societies purchased historic plantation homes and restored and furnished them for personal and public use.

Colonial Farms has been inspired by the Colonial Revival movement and the historical preservation efforts of Colonial Williamsburg.

With the Colonial Revival movement, interest in American colonial history flourished. Artists, writers, architects and antique collectors helped to bring such idealized images of early American colonial life to the wider public.

But at its heart, the Colonial Revival movement romanticized the American Revolution and the “Spirit of '76,” when patriots heroically fought for our independence in conjunction with America's mythical Founding Fathers.
