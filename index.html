The Colonial Heritage Farm is a 170–acre historic farm located in Westminster, Maryland. The farm's history dates back to the American Revolution and the Declaration of Independence in 1776. The Colonial Heritage Farm strives to provide the healthiest beef using sustainable farming practices and heritage breeds from the colonial era.

The farm raises Scottish Highland cattle, which is a heritage breed. Highlands are fondly known as the Grande Old Breed since they can be traced back to the 6th century. Heritage livestock breeds are typically historic and/or rare breeds that may have fallen out of favor with today's modern industrial farming practices.

Colonial Heritage Beef is the farm's independent brand of old-fashioned beef that is not only healthier and more nutritious, but also flavorful and delicious to eat.

The Colonial Heritage Inn is a historic boutique inn offering private accommodations for rest and relaxation.

Mission

The Colonial Heritage farm’s overall mission is to help preserve our agricultural heritage and produce healthier beef. To that end, the farm has four main objectives:

  • Raising heritage livestock – the farm raises Scottish Highland cattle, which is a heritage breed, and safeguards this breed's genetic diversity
  • Creating and marketing an independent brand of nutritious beef – the farm markets natural and healthier grass-fed heritage beef.
  • Utilization of sustainable agricultural practices – the farm utilizes pasture based livestock systems that protect our environment and also provide health benefits to both humans and animals
  • Historic preservation – the farm continues to preserve its history by maintaining a historic stone 1700s farm house, a 1700s stone smoke house, a historic bank barn built in 1888, and various other historic barns and outbuildings
