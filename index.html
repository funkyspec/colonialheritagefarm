Overview

The Colonial Heritage Farm is a 170–acre historic farm located in Westminster, Maryland that endeavors to preserve our agricultural heritage and the "Spirit of 1776."

The Farm raises Scottish Highland Cattle, a heritage breed known as the Grande Old breed tracing its roots back to the 6th century. Heritage livestock breeds are typically historic and/or rare breeds that may have fallen out of favor with today's modern industrial farming practices. Promoting historic livestock breeds and safeguarding their genetic diversity is an important Farm objective.

Marketing heritage farm products helps to reestablish the demand for these historic livestock breeds.  Furthermore, the Farm's heritage beef is not only produced locally, but raised more naturally and more nutritionally because of sustainable pasture based farming practices.

The Colonial Heritage Farm also focuses on historic preservation. The Farm has deep historic roots dating back to the American Colonial period and the American Revolution in the 1700s. Significant effort continues to this day to maintain and preserve this historic colonial farm as well as to safeguard its agricultural heritage.

Our Mission

The Colonial Heritage Farm’s overall mission is to help preserve our agricultural heritage. To that end, the Farm has four main objectives:

  • Preservation of Heritage Livestock – the Farm raises Scottish Highland Cattle and safeguards this breed's genetic diversity
  • Sale of Healthy and Locally Grown Farm Products – the Farm markets natural and healthy old-fashioned beef.
  • Utilization of Sustainable Agricultural Practices – the Farm utilizes pasture based livestock systems that protect our environment and also provide health benefits to both humans and animals
  • Historic Preservation – the Farm continues its efforts to preserve a historic stone house and smoke house built c.1781, a historic bank barn built in 1888, and various other barns and outbuildings
