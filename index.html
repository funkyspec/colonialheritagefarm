Overview

The Colonial Heritage Farm is a historic farm located in Westminster, Maryland that endeavors to preserve our agricultural heritage.

The Farm raises and markets heritage livestock breeds. Heritage livestock breeds are typically historic and/or rare breeds that may have fallen out of favor with today's modern industrial farming practices. Promoting historic livestock breeds and safeguarding their genetic diversity is an important Farm objective.

Marketing heritage farm products helps to reestablish the demand for these historic livestock breeds.  Furthermore, the Farm's heritage beef, heritage lamb and heritage eggs are not only produced locally, but they are grown more naturally and more nutritionally since they are raised utilizing sustainable pasture based farming practices.

The Colonial Heritage Farm also focuses on historic preservation. The Farm has deep historic roots dating back to the American Colonial period and the American Revolution in the 1700s. Significant effort continues to this day to maintain and preserve this historic colonial farm as well as to safeguard its agricultural heritage.

Our Mission

The Colonial Heritage Farm’s overall mission is to help preserve our agricultural heritage. To that end, the Farm has four main objectives:

  • Preservation of Heritage Livestock Breeds - the Farm works to safeguard and maintain historic and heirloom livestock breeds and their genetic diversity
  • Sale of Healthy and Locally Grown Heritage Farm Products - the Farm markets natural, nutritional and pasture raised heritage meats and eggs
  • Utilization of Sustainable Agricultural Practices - the Farm utilizes pasture based livestock systems that protect our environment and also provide health benefits to both humans and animals
  • Preservation of a Historical Farm - the Farm continues its efforts to preserve the farm's agricultural heritage including a historic stone house and smoke house built c.1781, a historic bank barn built in 1888 and various other barns and outbuildings
