Colonial Farms is part of the growing "Craft Beef" movement. The beef industry is currently undergoing a huge transformation. There is a significant resurgence of small family farms raising specialty beef breeds and creating unique tastes of beef for sale at local restaurants and at local farmers markets. This industry transformation is actually very similar to the explosion that occurred in the markets for specialty cheeses and craft beers.

As a result, Colonial Farms is focused on producing and marketing a unique type of Craft Beef referred to as "Highland Grass Beef." Craft Beef is often described in the following manner:

  1. Craft Beef is a premium beef from a specialty breed of cattle, like a Heritage breed, that is not cross bred. Heritage breeds are historic breeds that are smaller and slower growing that have fallen out of favor in today's industrial farming business.
  2. Craft Beef is healthier and more nutritious because it is typically nutrient rich, lower in cholesterol, lower in calories, free of antibiotics and free of artificial growth hormones, among various other benefits.
  3. Craft Beef is raised using sustainable agricultural practices and is 100% natural, pasture raised and grass-fed.
  4. Craft Beef is raised on family farms in small batches, not on large industrial farms.
  5. Craft Beef is raised humanely, under less stressful conditions. Less stressful beef tastes better and is more tender.
Highland Grass Beef

Highland Grass Beef is healthier and more nutritious for consumers. The beef is also flavorful and delicious. As a result of the Highland breed’s thick coat of long hair, the beef is naturally lean.

Highland Grass Beef is generally considered more of a premium grass-fed beef since it is rich in taste even when grass finished. Highland Grass Beef has old fashioned beef qualities that ensure tenderness and succulence with a very distinctive flavor.
