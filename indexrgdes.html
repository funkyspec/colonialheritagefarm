Colonial Heritage Cattle Company

The Colonial Heritage Farm is a 170–acre historic farm located in Westminster, Maryland. The farm's history dates back to the American Revolution and the Declaration of Independence in 1776.

The Farm raises Scottish Highland Cattle which is a heritage breed. Highlands are fondly known as the Grande Old Breed since they can be traced back to the 6th century. Heritage livestock breeds are typically historic and/or rare breeds that may have fallen out of favor with today's modern industrial farming practices.

The Colonial Heritage Farm also markets an independent brand of traditional beef that is not only old-fashioned, but is also flavorful and nutritious.

Our Mission

The Colonial Heritage Farm’s overall mission is to help preserve our agricultural heritage. To that end, the Farm has four main objectives:

  • Raising heritage livestock – the Farm raises Scottish Highland Cattle and safeguards this breed's genetic diversity
  • Creating and marketing of an independent brand of beef – the Farm markets natural and healthy old-fashioned beef.
  • Utilization of Sustainable Agricultural Practices – the Farm utilizes pasture based livestock systems that protect our environment and also provide health benefits to both humans and animals
  • Historic Preservation – the Farm continues to preserve its history by maintaining and using a historic stone 1700s farm house, a stone smoke house, a historic bank barn built in 1888, and various other barns and outbuildings
